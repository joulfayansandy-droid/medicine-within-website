## WHAT THIS WORK IS — AND IS NOT
This is not couples therapy.
This is not communication coaching.
This is not a sexual performance space.
This is embodied, tantric, and relational work —
where the body, nervous system, and relational field are met together.
We work with:
• presence instead of polarity games
• truth instead of strategy
• desire instead of obligation
• boundaries instead of blame
• intimacy instead of performance
The goal is not harmony.
The goal is aliveness, honesty, and safety — simultaneously.

## WHO THIS WORK IS FOR
This container is for couples who:
• Love each other, but feel stuck in recurring patterns
• Experience shutdown, conflict, or distance around intimacy
• Feel desire fading — or becoming complicated
• Want to explore deeper intimacy without breaking trust
• Are navigating transitions (commitment, opening, parenting, separation, healing)
• Have touched Tantra or Temple work and want integration together
• Want support that is deep, embodied, and precise
This work welcomes:
long-term couples
new couples
mixed-desire couples
monogamous or consciously non-monogamous dynamics
What matters is willingness — not perfection.

## WHAT BRINGS COUPLES HERE
Couples often arrive when:
• Communication keeps looping without resolution
• One partner leads, the other withdraws
• Desire feels mismatched or unsafe to talk about
• Intimacy has become mechanical — or absent
• Old wounds are triggered through closeness
• Power dynamics feel unspoken but present
• They want to grow together instead of apart
This work does not choose sides.
It listens to the relationship itself.

## HOW WE WORK WITH COUPLES
We work slowly, relationally, and with deep attunement.
Sessions may include:
• Embodied presence & nervous system regulation
• Tantric practices for intimacy, polarity, and truth
• Boundary and consent work (spoken and felt)
• Relational inquiry (attachment, protection patterns, desire)
• Ritual moments for repair, truth-telling, or reorientation
• Practices to bring intimacy back into daily life
Nothing is forced.
Nothing is rushed.
Everything is invitational.
This is not about fixing the relationship.
It’s about meeting it honestly — and seeing what becomes possible.

## WHAT CHANGES THROUGH THIS WORK
Couples often experience:
• Deeper emotional safety
• More honest communication without escalation
• Clearer boundaries and desires
• Renewed intimacy and attraction
• Less projection, more responsibility
• Greater capacity to stay present in discomfort
• A felt sense of “we can meet this together”
Sometimes couples deepen.
Sometimes they clarify.
Both are acts of love.

## HOW THIS DIFFERS FROM GROUP WORK
Group spaces activate insight.
Couples work re-patterns the relational field itself.
This is why couples choose private work:
after Tantra or Temple experiences
during relationship crossroads
when intimacy feels tender or volatile
when they want depth without exposure
This work is discreet, held, and deeply respectful.
